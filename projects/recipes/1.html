Seven Layer Casserole

The recipe I chose was the Seven Layer Cassorole. My family has been having this for dinner for a couple months now. We started off having it for dinner on normal days and over time we have started making it for guests and family members as well. It is a good dish to make for people who come over because ithas a lot of different components so if someone doesn't like a specific food, there are other parts of the dish they may enjoy. Last summer, we made it for my aunt who doen't eat meat. Because it is a dish that we can easily adjust to dietary restrictions, we were able to make her portion without meat and she still expressed to us that it was "A very delicious meal." I think it is a convienient dish because a lot of the ingredients are things you can find pre packaged in cans, boxes, or bags at the grocery store. It is time efficiant and all you have to do is warm up those ingredients and add them to the cassorole. Overall it is a somewhat recent addition to my family's meal plan and we enjoy having it.

Preperation time: 45 min Serving size: 4-6 servings
Ingredients Amount Instructions
Olive Oil 2 Tablespoons Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.
Ground Beef 1 Pound Heat olive oil and beef in large skillet and cook beef on medium heat until browned (about 5-6 minutes).
Taco seasoning 2 heaping tablespoons Sprinkle taco seasoning and a pinch of salt and pepper then set aside.
Two bags of microwave white rice 8.5-ounces Add layer of rice to 2 1/2-to 3-quart baking dish.
Can of ranch style beans One 15-ounce can Add beans, corn, tomatoes, and green chiles to the dish.
Frozen fire-roasted corn 1 cup
Can of tomatoes and green chiles One 15-ounce can
Grated Monterey Jack Cheese 1 1/2 cups Add cheese to top and bake in oven until cheese is fully melted and it is hot throughout (about 18-20 min).

Tips/Substitutions

  • In my family, we use a non-stick pan to make the beef in, so if you also use a non-stick pan, you don't need to use olive oil before cooking the beef because it will not stick to the pan.
  • Another substitution my family makes is that we often use more than a heaping tablespoon of taco seasoning. It may sound like a lot, but sometimes the flavor can get a little lost.
  • I would reccomend tasting the seasoning after stirring it in and adding more accordingly.
  • In my family, we don't really eat green chiles so we usually leave that out.
  • We also like to use sweet corn instead of fir-roasted corn.
  • You can customize the ingrediants to you and your family's likings as well as leave some ingredients out.
